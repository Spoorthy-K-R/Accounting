Here's a comprehensive financial analysis of NVIDIA (NVDA) based on the provided financial statements.

**1. Financial Trends Analysis**

*   **Revenue:**  NVIDIA has experienced explosive revenue growth over the past four years. Revenue has increased from $26.97 billion in 2022 to $130.50 billion in 2025. This represents a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 70%. This is a significant acceleration, especially considering the size of the company.

*   **Profitability:**  Net income has followed a similar trajectory, increasing from $9.75 billion in 2022 to $72.88 billion in 2025.  This indicates strong operating leverage.

    *   **Margins:** Gross profit margin has increased significantly from 64.9% in 2022 to 75.0% in 2025. This is a very positive trend, suggesting increasing pricing power and/or decreasing cost of goods sold as a percentage of revenue.  Operating margin has also increased dramatically, reflecting improved efficiency and scale.

*   **Debt:**  Net debt has fluctuated. It increased from $7.56 billion in 2023 to $11.06 billion in 2024, then decreased to $10.27 billion in 2025. While debt has increased, it is important to note that the company's cash position has also increased significantly, and the debt levels are relatively low compared to the company's earnings.

*   **Cash Flow:**  Free cash flow (FCF) has grown substantially, from $8.13 billion in 2022 to $60.85 billion in 2025. This robust FCF generation provides NVIDIA with significant financial flexibility.

*   **Capital Structure:**  NVIDIA has been actively repurchasing its own shares.  The repurchase of capital stock was -$33.71 billion in 2025, -$9.53 billion in 2024, and -$10.04 billion in 2023. This suggests management believes the stock is undervalued and is returning capital to shareholders.  Share count has decreased slightly due to these repurchases.

*   **Working Capital:** Working capital has increased significantly, reflecting the growth in the business.

*   **Capital Expenditures:** Capital expenditures have increased from $976 million in 2022 to $3.24 billion in 2025, reflecting investments in growth.

**Significant Changes, Anomalies, and Turning Points:**

*   **2024-2025 Acceleration:** The most significant turning point is the acceleration of revenue and profit growth between 2024 and 2025. This suggests a major shift in market demand or NVIDIA's competitive position.
*   **Margin Expansion:** The substantial increase in gross and operating margins is a key positive development.
*   **Increased Share Repurchases:** The large share repurchase programs indicate management's confidence in the company's future prospects.
